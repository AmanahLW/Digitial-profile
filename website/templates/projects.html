Projects

A pipeline for identifying variants in zebrafish, April 2022-August 2022

· A Snakemake pipeline which implemented singularity containers, python/shell scripts and YAML was developed. · The singularity containers packaged 3 variant-calling tools (e.g., GATK), an aligner and other tools to identify variants in the zebrafish (model) from exome sequence alignments in BAM format. · Natural mutations were found using a python script and annotated. · Python wrangled variants to obtain the correct format for statistical analysis. · Natural variants from several hundred samples are to be deposited in the European Variation Archive.

Integrating transcriptomics and proteomics data from ovarian cancer samples to study tumour microenvironment, December 2021

· Experience with R for the analysis of transcriptomic data was highlighted by studying the tumour microenvironment. · The difference in gene expression of a biomarker for normal and cancer samples was explored. Also, the correlation between transcript and protein levels of the biomarker was visualised. · Also, genes that have a transcript that significantly correlates with protein levels and genes with significant differential gene expression between cancer and normal samples were identified. · A hierarchical cluster analysis was performed using transcript expression data.

Evaluate a machine learning tool to diagnose Crohn’s disease using metabolomic data, November 2021

· Experience with omics analysis was gained from testing and training a machine learning model. · The trends of total ion chromatography for each sample with 50 observations were explored. · Also, scaled metabolomic data from 23 samples were pre-processed using PCA. · The pre-processed metabolomic patient data was utilised for training and testing an ensemble of SVM classifiers. · 3 metrics were used to evaluate the reliability of models generated by different bootstrap and ensemble numbers.
