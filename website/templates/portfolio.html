Case Studies

A pipeline for identifying variants in zebrafish, April 2022-August 2022

Aim:
To fill the gap in the availability of zebrafish (ZF) variants in a public database.
To analyse variants and record the analysis in my thesis.
Objective:
To use snakemake to build a standard germline variant calling pipeline.
To examine the distribution of variants, quality scores, read depth, genotypes and variant consequences.
To examine the population structure using a PCA.

Integrating transcriptomics and proteomics data from ovarian cancer samples to study the tumour microenvironment, December 2021

Aim:
The investigation will address the effect of high-grade serous ovarian cancer (HGSOC) on the expression of transcript and protein molecules located in the extracellular matrix of the tumour microenvironment.
Objective:
To perform an exploratory analysis of the data.
To perform statistical testing to identify genes with a significant correlation to proteins.
As well as, statistical testing will be used to identify genes with a significant differential expression.

Evaluate a machine learning tool to diagnose Crohn’s disease using metabolomic data, November 2021

Aim:
To evaluate a machine learning (ML) model for the diagnosis of patients with Crohn’s disease, using metabolomic data generated by GC-MS.
Objective:
To train the ML model using data from several sample types to find the model with the most accurate diagnosis. The sample types included breath, urine, blood and feaces.
